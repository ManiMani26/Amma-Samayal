Cooking Tips 👨🏽‍🍳

1. Plan Ahead 🍴

  • List all recipes you want to make (sweets, snacks, savories).
  • Check pantry stocks: flours, lentils, rice, ghee, spices, jaggery, sugar, and dry fruits.
  • Prep in batches: For example, soak dals, grind coconut, or make sugar syrup a day before.

2. Master Your Frying🍴

  • Oil temperature is key: 160–180°C for most snacks like murukku, thattai, or pakoda. Too hot → burns; too cold → soggy snacks.
  • Use a deep, heavy pan for even frying.
  • Fry small batches to maintain oil temperature and crispiness.
  • Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

3. Sweets Preparation Tips🍴

  • Sugar Syrups: For jalebi, boondi, or laddu, check one-string or two-string consistency using the thread test.
  • Ghee/Roasting: Roast gram flour, semolina, or coconut evenly to avoid raw taste in sweets.
  • Storage: Cool sweets completely before storing in airtight containers to maintain freshness.

4. Time-Saving Tricks🍴

  • Use ready-made flours or mixes for complex snacks if short on time (e.g., besan or rava).
  • Make sweets/snacks in parallel: While sugar syrup boils, shape laddu or prep murukku dough.
  • Use stand mixers or hand mixers to make dough quickly for snacks like murukku or ribbon pakoda.

5. Enhance Flavors🍴

  • Fresh spices: Cardamom, saffron, and nutmeg make a big difference in sweets like payasam, badam halwa, and masala paal.
  • Ghee quality: Pure ghee enhances aroma and taste in most Diwali sweets.
  • Dry fruits: Roast nuts lightly to intensify flavor before adding to burfi, laddus, or kesari.

6. Avoid Common Mistakes🍴

  • Don’t overcrowd the frying pan – snacks won’t cook evenly.
  • Don’t add water directly to hot sugar syrup – it may crystallize.
  • Monitor sweets while reducing milk for halwa or payasam – milk can easily burn.
  • eep fire safety in mind when frying large batches of snacks.

7. Presentation & Storage🍴

  • Use decorative plates or boxes for gifting sweets.
  • Separate containers for different snacks to prevent sogginess.
  • Cool completely before sealing – warm sweets can spoil faster.

8. Quick Fixes🍴

  • Soggy snacks: Re-fry briefly on medium heat to restore crispness.
  • Burnt sugar syrup: Start fresh; burnt sugar will taste bitter.
  • Sticky burfi: Dust lightly with powdered sugar or ghee to prevent sticking.
